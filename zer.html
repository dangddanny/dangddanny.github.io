Zer

vr game | 2017

Role: Designer, Programmer

“How can we use virtual reality to teach kids how to spot fake news?”

Research

The 2016 U.S Presidential election made the general public more aware about the damaging effects of fake news. Around this time was when my partner Kate and I were choosing a topic for our senior thesis. We became fixated on the issue of fake news and researched different ways we could address it. We decided to make Generation Z, the generation following the Millenials, our target audience. This demographic is the first generation of true digital natives. They grew up with iPhones and iPads and get their news off of social media. They have a tendency to share content they care about, and this makes them susceptible to spreading misinformation on platforms like Snapchat, Twitter, and Facebook.


2016 also brought us the rise of virtual reality. Many tech companies were getting into the field, building their own headsets, and trying to find new uses for it. We chose to make Zer in VR to:

  • Represent news, not online or in print, but spatially
  • Find where VR can intersect journalism
  • Use a form that youth will eventually be used to

A virtual reality game to teach kids the skills they need to recognize fake news.
